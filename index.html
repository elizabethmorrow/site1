About Me

My drive to become an engineer stems from a love of problem-solving, and a passion for applying my skills to help others. My professional interests lie primarily in embedded software development, robotics and the human machine relationship.

Last Coop

I had 3 major projects while working at MedAvail, as well as daily testing and electrical CAD work. The Audio Test System is written in C# and used Fast Fourier Transforms to analyze audio signal quality and determine if a handset passed quality control. I also designed and wrote the Embedded Log Converter (also C#), a parsing app for the data packets received from the microcontrollers in the MedCentres to make them readable, and translatable. During my coop I also contributed to the Utility software suite, helping redesign the GUI and integrating a version of the log converter to translate machine logs in real time.

Ongoing Projects

Solar Car Control System

This winter I'm very excited to take a lead role in developing the firmware for the 11th Midnight Sun Solar car. Currently I am working on the CAN communication on the main board, and will soon be putting together an electrical harness to simulate and test the start-up procedures of the solar car. Ask me about how it's going!

At Midnight Sun we develop primarily in C, using CodeComposer for an IDE. Right now the main priority of the Electrical team is getting the new code and the new board revisions up and running, as we aim to compete in the World Solar Challenge in Australia 2015.
