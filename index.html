About Me

My decision to become an engineer stemmed from a love of problem-solving, and a passion for applying my skills to help others. In the summer of 2011, I worked as a lab assistant at the University of British Columbia, following my interest in the human machine relationship, specifically with regards to health care. It was here that I was first exposed to the role of mechatronics in everyday life, as prosthetic limbs and modifications to homes to accomodate disabilities. Since then, I have been actively developing and applying my skills as a multidiciplinary engineer, through my work in industry, in class, and extra-curricular activities and projects. Work

I had 3 major projects while working at MedAvail, as well as daily testing and electrical CAD work. The Audio Test System is written in C# and used Fast Fourier Transforms to analyze audio signal quality and determine if a handset passed quality control. I also designed and wrote the Embedded Log Converter (also C#), a parsing app for the data packets received from the microcontrollers in the MedCentres to make them readable, and translatable. During my coop I also contributed to the Utility software suite, helping redesign the GUI and integrating a version of the log converter to translate machine logs in real time.
