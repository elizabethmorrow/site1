About Me

My decision to become an engineer stemmed from a love of problem-solving, and a passion for applying my skills to help others. In the summer of 2011, I worked as a lab assistant at the University of British Columbia, following my interest in the human machine relationship, specifically with regards to health care. It was here that I was first exposed to the role of mechatronics in everyday life, as prosthetic limbs and modifications to homes to accomodate disabilities. Since then, I have been actively developing and applying my skills as a multidiciplinary engineer, through my work in industry, in class, and extra-curricular activities and projects.
